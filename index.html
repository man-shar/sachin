Sachin Tendulkar: The Greatest of All Time
The task of proving that Sachin Tendulkar seems easy due to the magnitude of what he accomplished and his stature in India, but when you start to look for contenders, there are a lot of them. At first I began to look into older players like Sir Don Bradman, Viv Richards, etc. But then I soon realised, Sachin and others of his generation had long surpassed their predecessors jut by their longevity and the sheer number of matches they played. Sir Don Bradman played just 52 test matches in his entire career. Sachin played 200. He never even played ODIs. Sachin, however played 464 of them.

Although the older players do not have that much of an effect on Sachin's place in history, I will still display their statistics in the following charts just to prove my point of why it doesn't matter who we compare Sachin to. Looking at Wisden's 100, I decided on five batsmen who I feel would be the best ones to compare Sachin to in order to validate my argument. They are- Sir Don Bradman, Sir Viv Rochards, Ricky Ponting, Rahul Dravid and Jacques Kallis. Let's begin!

Career Averages

One of the most basic things which cricket gurus compare batsman by is their career batting averages. It not only gives an idea about the players growth but also his consistency. If he is ever a bad performer, it shows up in his average. His total runs still increase, but his average falls.

Sir Don Bradman is incomparable here if seen by absolute numbers. He retired with an average of 99.75. Insane. But the fact that he played just 53 test matche in his career, is a major negative point. He was a brilliant and a very consistent batsman, but just did not play for long so as to fairly compare. THe other batsman are all very similar in terms of their growth and averages through the careers with all aveaging around the 45+ mark. Thus the criteria to judge here is longevity. Sachin wins in longevity outright. He played 664 international matches in his career. Most by any player ever. The second in out pool is RIcky Ponting, with 543. More than a 100 matches less than Sachin. Thus here, the criteria boils down to longevity and hence Sachin leads.
